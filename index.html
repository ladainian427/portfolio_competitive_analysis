Competitive Analysis

An analysis of 10 web portfolio's based on brand, functionality, interaction, and navigation.

1. Hovie

URL:http://hovie.com/recent.shtml

Date Looked At: Jan.15.2012.

Brand

Artistic - designed by someone who isn't just a programmer, has art background.

Visual - Pictures and colors are strictly visual, easy on the eyes.

Modern - Style looks up to date with today's art.

Functionality Shows a minimal design of the logo for each item. Does a good job displaying the design but not explaining what each thing is.
Interaction Mouse over highlights what your mouse is on. Only clicking with links and pictures. Zoom's when you click on the picture.
Navigation Sidebar for navigation but doesn't explain what the tabs are. Impossible to go back to something unless you memorized it.

2. The Church of London

URL: http://www.thechurchoflondon.com/

Date Looked At: Jan.15.2012.

Brand

White - Uses an all white background, allows you to really focus on content.

Classy - Nothing childish, sticks with simple fonts and colors, nothing flashy

Stylish - Simple content but still able to portray a sense of class and style. Reminds me of something you'd see in New York.
Functionality Doesn't show the designs but shows clients and descriptions. You have to go further to see the designs. Does provide details on the home page.
Interaction Search bar, mouse over, horizontal side scroll for transitions. Also uses coverflow to display the different samples.
Navigation Has subtabs to show you which tab you're on. Easy to learn, hard to get lost. Organized by recent projects, blogs, and clients.
