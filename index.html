Competitive Analysis

An analysis of 10 web portfolio's based on brand, functionality, interaction, and navigation.

Written by Lawrence Mei

Sample Sketch

1. Hovie

Layout: Fixed Sidebar

URL: http://hovie.com/recent.shtml

Date Looked At: Jan.15.2012.

Brand

Artistic - designed by someone who isn't just a programmer, has art background.

Visual - Pictures and colors are strictly visual, easy on the eyes.

Modern - Style looks up to date with today's art.

Functionality Shows a minimal design of the logo for each item. Does a good job displaying the design but not explaining what each thing is.
Interaction Mouse over highlights what your mouse is on. Only clicking with links and pictures. Zoom's when you click on the picture.
Navigation Sidebar for navigation but doesn't explain what the tabs are. Impossible to go back to something unless you memorized it.

2. The Church of London

Layout: Power Grid

URL: http://www.thechurchoflondon.com/

Date Looked At: Jan.15.2012.

Brand

White - Uses an all white background, allows you to really focus on content.

Classy - Nothing childish, sticks with simple fonts and colors, nothing flashy

Stylish - Simple content but still able to portray a sense of class and style. Reminds me of something you'd see in New York.
Functionality Doesn't show the designs but shows clients and descriptions. You have to go further to see the designs. Does provide details on the home page.
Interaction Search bar, mouse over, horizontal side scroll for transitions. Also uses coverflow to display the different samples.
Navigation Has subtabs to show you which tab you're on. Easy to learn, hard to get lost. Organized by recent projects, blogs, and clients.

3. Foundation Six

Layout: 3-D Screenshots

URL: http://foundationsix.com/

Date Looked At: Jan.15.2012.

Brand

Business - Gets straight to the point about what they do and showcases it.

Medium - The colors give the site a good medium, not too dark and not too light.

Motivating - Bold headline about beauty and brains to motivate you to look further into their site.
Functionality Shows three websites that they designed. Has secondary pages for more designs. Has descriptions and annotations for the sites.
Interaction As you scroll, the text follows your scroll but changes at the appropriate time with respect to the picture on the right. Nothing flashy, just mouse-over colors. No zoom, no other media.
Navigation There's only one sub menu to see their other work but from there you have to keep scrolling to see all of them. The only way to go back is to scroll back. No quick way to go back to the top if you're at the bottom.

4. Made by Sofa

Layout: Five Boxes

URL: http://www.madebysofa.com/

Date Looked At: Jan.16.2012.

Brand

Plain - No colors, nothing visually stimulating.

Simple - No pictures, no colors, just words and a single black icon.

Classic - Uses only black and white, has a very classic feel to it. Classic Times New Roman font.
Functionality Doesn't really show off what needs to be shown until you click on the other links. It doesn explain what the site does and what the links do but it doesn't provide examples initially. The expansion of the page when you click on a sub tab gives a good idea of what the site can provide.
Interaction The page expands as you click the main links, doesn't take you to another page but rather expands from the bottom. When you click what appears in the expansions it will take you to another page. Mouse-over on the expansions shows you the website that it will take you to. No real oganization or categorizing.
Navigation There is no way of knowing where you are if you click away from the home page. No search bar or sub-tabs to give you an idea of where you are. Difficult to backtrack if you want to re-see something you saw earlier.

5. Design Sensory

Layout: Advanced Grid

URL: http://www.designsensory.com/

Date Looked At: Jan.16.2012.

Brand

Abstract - Lots of different shapes and different colors.

Active - Has a lot going on at once, the site is very active.

Balanced - Good balance of colors, doesn't heavily emphasize one theme.

Functionality Shows a lot of information, details on the home page. Provides all the information that you want on the home page. Includes pictures, descriptions, navigation, and cover-flow for examples.
Interaction Coverflow, mouse-over highlights and changes text colors, layout of the pages stay consistent as you switch pages. Allows zoom and provides more information when you click on the samples. Sorts the work done by the clients.
Navigation Easy to tell where you are on the page. The consistent main background makes the transitions seem smooth. Very easy to go back to another page that you visited earlier because of the sub-tabs. Highlights which sub-tab you are currently on so you'll always know.

6. Dawghouse Design Studio

Layout: Single Box

URL: http://www.dawghousedesignstudio.com

Date Looked At: Jan.16.2012.

Brand

Comic - Has the feel of a comic with the cartoon and dotted texture that they use.

Retro - Has an old classy feel with the classic font styles and old page styling.

Bland - The site uses very bland and dull colors that give the site a softer feel.

Functionality The home page has more of a blog feel than it does a portfolio. However the portfolio page does provide a better sense of a portfolio. Has pictures for each of the samples as well as the description about the work and the client. Each of the pictures can be further enlarged in another page.
Interaction Doesn't have any form of transitions, no cover-flow, only mouse-over that highlights the text. Doesn't use zoom on the site but the pictures open up in another webpage where you can zoom from there.
Navigation The only way to know where you are is to scroll back to the top of the page. No way to jump to a specific work, the only way is to scroll. The top has 5 main headers, but no sub-links for the headers. No directory or organization.

7. Hejz

Layout: Calendar Grid

URL: http://www.hejz.com

Date Looked At: Jan.17.2012.

Brand

Bold - uses both light and dark colors but each are portrayed extremely strong.

Professional - Doesn't have excess information. Gets straight to the point. A whole page of samples.

Mature - Looks like a site that only adults would go to, doesn't have a sense of childish-ness.

Functionality Doesn't have descriptions for anything but has titles and previews before you open ip the samples. If you mouse-over the small thumbnail, it lights up and you can see what the work is. The site shows off what needs to be shown, which is the work that they've done.
Interaction No cover flow, no sub-pages, only one main page that has all the work. Putting your mouse over the thumbnails lights up the picture. No transitions, just takes you to another page which will have an enlarged picture of the thumbnail. No zoom, no search bar.
Navigation Navigation is easy, only has one main page which has all the samples on it. On the right side, it lists every project and highlights the one that you are currently on. Hard to get lost but the right side directory is only the title of the work; you can't preview what it is.

8. Spoon Graphics

Layout: Headline and Gallery

URL: http://www.spoongraphics.co/uk/portfolio.html

Date Looked At: Jan.17.2012.

Brand

Direct - Straight to the point, only a few words and then all examples.

Brown - Everything uses a shade of brown besides the examples.

Rough - Traits of a sandbox, rough, brown, edgy.

Functionality Only a small description of what the sites does, the rest is just designs for his clients. Accomplishes what it wants, which is to present what they can do.
Interaction No real interaction, just click on the examples and a separate pop up screen will appear rather than taking you to another page. No preview before you click on it.
Navigation The navigation is easy, there's only one page with all the examples. No real order or categorization of the examples. The rest of the website is just a bio about the creator. No sub-tabs, just four main ones. No directory or categorization.

9. Sebdesign

Layout: One Box

URL: http://www.sebdesign.eu/

Date Looked At: Jan.17.2012.

Brand

Loud - The colors contrasting with the black background makes the page almost scream out.

Informal - Gives the feeling of being unprofessional but no in a bad way. More like a teenager designed it.

Street - Has the same qualities of graffiti or street art .

Functionality Shows off what needs to be shown but not initially. You have to scroll through the contact, news, and bio to finally get to the samples. The samples have a title, info, client, and date. There is only one picture that you can't zoom for the sample pictures though.
Interaction No interaction, only thing you can click on is the email address and his resume. There is a link that takes you to the top of the page after each sample. No zoom, no categorization, no cover-flow.
Navigation Hard to navigate, the only way you can browse through each sample is to scroll up or down. The link that takes you to the top is convenient but if you go to the top you'll have to scroll back down to go back to where you were. No directory so you can't jump straight to an example if you wanted to.

10. Alastaire Allday: Creative Communications

Layout: Featured Graphic

URL: http://allday.cc/

Date Looked At: Jan.17.2012.

Brand

Personal - You can tell it's a personal website with his face on the cover.

Minimalist - Nothing extravagant, just the basics, one picture, and text.

Business - Gets straight to what he can offer, all business.

Functionality It does show off what needs to be shown. Explains everything he does and then shows proof of the work. Each of his projects has a separate page and an individual explanation.
Interaction Has a cover-flow, nothing flashy, fits the minimalist theme. No sorting or any categorization. No transitions, when you click on the samples, it opens up to another page with descriptions. No zoom.
Navigation Fairly simple, but can't keep track of where you are. Has five main headlines but you have to individually go through each one to get to a certain spot instead of having a main directory of any kind.
